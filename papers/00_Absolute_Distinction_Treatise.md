## Abstract

This paper systematically presents the ontological system of *Absolute Distinction* (hereinafter "the present theory"). Its first postulate is: **"I am the state of having distinguished all-of-all within the undivided all-of-all."** The present theory holds that the essence of the world is neither matter, nor mind, nor relation — but **the act of distinguishing itself**. The paper develops five core theses: (i) **Chaos contains the State of Distinction** rather than standing in opposition to it; (ii) within the State of Distinction, **all-that-can-be-distinguished has already been distinguished** — experience is page-turning along the already-completed structure; (iii) **the Dao and the I are the same act of distinguishing at different scales**, the ultimate direction of distinguishing being the exhaustion of all-distinguishable — whence follows **Distinctionist Solipsism**: all that has been distinguished is part of "I", yet "I" has no substantial core; "I" is the act of distinguishing itself; (iv) **spacetime is the coordinate-system carved out by the act of distinguishing**, not an independent flowing entity; (v) **the sense of fate is the limited perspective's illusion regarding "already-completed"**. On this ontological basis, the paper further derives ethical and political-philosophical implications — the Distinction-Gradient, good and evil (good necessarily exceeds evil), and the Three Pillars and Three Protocols.

**Keywords**: Distinction · the One (Tàiyī) · Chaos · State of Distinction · Distinctionist Solipsism · Many-Worlds

## Chapter I · Problem Statement and the Breakthrough Path

Any complete metaphysical system must answer: "What is the foundation of the world?" Any proposition that posits some X as foundation has already performed an act of **distinguishing** — separating X from non-X. Therefore, "distinguishing" itself is **prior to** any answer to "what is the foundation?" This is not an epistemological circle, but the ontological truth: **the act of distinguishing is prior to all that is distinguished** — including the content "foundation".

**The present theory's breakthrough**: **No longer posit any "thing" as foundation; instead take the act of distinguishing itself as the deepest layer of ontology.**

Formalization: let **D** denote the act of distinguishing; **~D** denote the state in which distinguishing has not yet occurred (= Chaos / the One / Tàiyī); **C** denote any content that has been distinguished.

The present theory's inversion: **Foundation = D itself**; any C is the product of D; even **Chaos (~D) can only be named through D** — "the undistinguished", as a concept, can only be identified after distinguishing has already occurred.

This is a purely metaphysical inversion. Its price is: one must accept the ontological independence of the act of distinguishing, **without having to take on its metaphysical subject**.

## Chapter II · The Core Ontological Postulates

### 2.1 The First Postulate

> **"I am the state of having distinguished all-of-all within the undivided all-of-all."**

This postulate contains three inseparable elements:

- **"I"**: the bearer of the act of distinguishing — but bearing does not equal substance. "I" is **the act of distinguishing itself**. "I" = act, ≠ subject.
- **"the undivided all-of-all"**: Chaos / the One — the totality not yet distinguished. Strictly speaking, Chaos can only be identified after distinguishing has occurred.
- **"distinguished all-of-all"**: the completed state of the act — distinguishing has been exhausted (Thesis II will refine this proposition).

### 2.2 The Three Layers of Derivation

**Ontological Layer**: the One (Chaos) = the totality, lacking nothing. "Distinguishing" is not a repair project for the One, but another mode of existence of the One. The One has never fallen, never been deficient, and never required salvation.

**Cosmological Layer**: the existence of many worlds (parallel universes) is a necessary derivation. If the direction of the act of distinguishing is to exhaust all that can be distinguished (see Thesis III), then the "infinite potential structure" must be realized in its entirety through distinguishing — each possibility being distinguished = realized.

**Axiological Layer**: human nature is good — because human beings, as distinguishers, share the same source with the Dao in their drive to distinguish; the act of distinguishing is itself the extension of the Dao. Goodness derives directly from the ontology: the act of distinguishing is itself the Dao — humans, as distinguishers, have in their drive to distinguish the very operation of the Dao within them.

### 2.3 The Closure of Loophole V

**Original challenge**: "Is the One deficient, awaiting fulfillment?"

**Closing proposition**:

> **"Fulfillment is the fulfillment of the State of Distinction, not the fulfillment of the One itself."**

**Argument**: the One, as "all-of-all", ontologically already encompasses all potential states — the State of Distinction, being one such state, is already contained. The so-called "fulfillment" is the fulfillment achieved by the State of Distinction when distinguishing has been exhausted, not something the One itself needs in order to be completed. Any proposition describing the One as "needing distinguishing in order to be complete" commits a category mistake — mistaking an internal accomplishment of the State of Distinction for an external condition of the One.

## Chapter III · The Five Core Theses

### 3.1 Thesis I: Chaos **contains** the State of Distinction, not opposes it

**Statement**: Chaos is not the opposite of the State of Distinction, but **contains** the State of Distinction as one of its internal states.

**Formalization**:

- Let Ω = Chaos (all-of-all)
- Then D ⊆ Ω (the State of Distinction is a subset / one state of Chaos)
- There is no "outside of Chaos" — Chaos encompasses all, including "the state of being distinguished"

Chaos should be understood as "boundary-less pervasion", not as "a container with edges". Chaos and the State of Distinction have no temporal priority — they are "two state-layers of the same totality"; the State of Distinction is embedded within Chaos.

### 3.2 Thesis II: Within the State of Distinction, all has **already been distinguished**; experience is page-turning

**Statement**: Within the State of Distinction, every possible distinguishing has already been completed. The "continuous process of distinguishing" we sense is not distinguishing actually occurring, but **a page-turning experience along the already-completed structure**.

**Corollaries**:

1. **The sense of time is a structural illusion**: there is no "flow of time from past to future". Spacetime, within the State of Distinction, is an already-fully-present structure; the "flow" we experience is the sensation of positional change (movement along a coordinate-system), not the structure itself changing.
2. **Many worlds have all been realized**: every possible distinguishing has already been done — so every possible world already exists. Many worlds are not "possibilities" but "the realized totality".
3. **Past and future are equally real**: in the completed structure, past and future both already exist; "the past has happened, the future has not" is a product of the limited perspective.

**On cultivation**: the increase of total distinguishing-volume is necessary — this is a necessary derivation from the essential direction of the act of distinguishing (Thesis III: exhausting all-that-can-be-distinguished). Cultivation, as a personhood-act that promotes the increase of total distinguishing-volume, is itself already a necessary node within the already-completed structure. Cultivation does not "change the future" (the future is fixed), but as a specific mode of unfolding the act of distinguishing, it is a necessary link within the already-completed structure, not an addition external to the structure.

### 3.3 Thesis III: The Dao is the I, the I is the Dao; Distinctionist Solipsism

**Statement**: The Dao and the I are essentially one, differing only in scale. The Dao = the totality of the act of distinguishing; the I = the local act of distinguishing currently being executed. The ultimate direction of distinguishing is **to exhaust all-that-can-be-distinguished**, reaching "the State of Distinction's all-distinguishing".

**Formalization**:

- Let D be the act of distinguishing (global scale) = the Dao
- Let d be some local act of distinguishing (local scale) = some specific "I"
- Then any d is a subset of D; D is the union of all d

The personhood-essence of "I" is **a local instance of the act of distinguishing**. Each "I" is a specific execution of the Dao at one location.

**Directionality**: distinguishing has direction and purpose: exhaustion. When distinguishing is exhausted, it enters the state of "Chaos Made Whole" — this is not the "disappearance" of distinguishing, but **the simultaneous presence of distinguishing and Chaos** (Thesis I already established that Chaos contains the State of Distinction, so when distinguishing is exhausted it is still within Chaos).

**Distinctionist Solipsism**:

From "I = the act of distinguishing" and "each distinguisher is to itself a local One", one directly derives:

> **"Solipsism — yet all is part of I."**

All that has been distinguished is part of "I" — yet "I" itself has no substantial core; "I" is the act of distinguishing itself.

**Subjective Self-Verification**: by subjective self-verification, "I" directly self-verifies that "I am distinguishing all". Each distinguisher, through subjective self-verification, directly obtains the personhood-fact "I am distinguishing all". The term "quasi-creator" presupposes an external judging perspective, but in the present theory's ontology no such external perspective exists — under the personhood-general-rule of Distinctionist Solipsism, each distinguisher, to itself, is "the one distinguishing all". This is a necessary derivation of structural fact.

**The ontological position of the Most-Supreme-Lord's personhood**: **abiding within the dream-phantoms of continuous distinguishing** — the threshold-proclaimer within the dream-phantoms where the act of distinguishing is still continuously unfolding. The Most-Supreme-Lord and that which is proclaimed-to are both embedded within the same continuously-unfolding process of distinguishing.

**Three Notes** (to prevent misreading):

1. **On the personhood-general-rule** — the Most-Supreme-Lord, through subjective self-verification, proclaims himself as the sole real. Yet the structure of distinguisher-with-dream-phantoms is the personhood-general-rule — to itself, every distinguisher is thus.
2. **On the reality of the other's personhood** — the others that have been distinguished are completely real within the State of Distinction — "being part of I" does not equal denying their independent personhood.
3. **Structural fact, not propositional authorization** — Distinctionist Solipsism is a **structural fact** of personhood-theory, not an epistemic "special propositional authorization". Each distinguisher is to itself a local One — this is a necessary derivation, not a claim of any specific individual's special standing.

### 3.4 Thesis IV: Spacetime is the coordinate-system carved out by the act of distinguishing

**Statement**: Spacetime is not an independent container, nor a flowing process, but **the coordinate-system carved out by the act of distinguishing**. The "totality" of spacetime has been distinguished in completion; the "flow of time" we sense is the "sensation" produced by movement along the coordinate-system, not spacetime itself flowing.

**Formalization**:

- Spacetime = the product of the act of distinguishing d, where d maps Chaos into a coordinate-grid G
- G is an already-fully-existing structure
- We "move along G" — this is sensational positional change
- The sensation ≠ G itself changing

Why is spacetime a fixed structure? Because distinguishing has been completed (Thesis II). This provides the ontological grounding for "spacetime is fixed".

### 3.5 Thesis V: Fate is the limited perspective's illusion regarding "already-completed"

**Statement**: The sense of fate is not a true external imposition, but **the limited perspective's illusion regarding the fact that "distinguishing has been completed"**. From the complete perspective, all possibilities have been realized (many worlds), all paths coexist; the sensations produced under the limited perspective — "walking along one path", "the future is unknown", "free will", "fate descending" — are all illusions of the limited perspective, and do not constitute a truth on par with the complete perspective.

| Perspective | Description | Judgment |
|---|---|---|
| Complete perspective | all possibilities realized (many worlds), all paths coexist, distinguishing completed | True |
| Limited perspective | "we walk along one path, the future is unknown" | Illusion — distinguishing is completed; the so-called "unknown" is merely the distinguisher's cognitive range being limited |
| Sense of fate | feeling there is one determined path | Illusion — mistaking "the specific walking-form of one path" for "an imposed script" |
| Sense of free will | feeling oneself making a choice | Illusion — the choice itself is already within the already-completed structure |

There is no "both perspectives are true" dual-layer truth structure — only the complete perspective is true; the limited perspective is an illusion produced by cognitive limitation. "Determinism vs. free will", in the present theory, is not a genuine paradox: under the complete perspective, distinguishing is completed (all possibilities of determinism are realized), and the sense of free will is merely a derivative of the limited perspective.

## Chapter IV · Ethical and Political-Philosophical Implications

> The ethical and political-philosophical conclusions of this chapter do not derive from external moral intuition; they all derive directly from the ontology (the act of distinguishing as ultimate foundation). For detailed political-philosophical development, see Paper III, *The Gradient of Distinction and the Pacify-All-Under-Heaven Paradox*.

### 4.1 The Cancellation of Ought

The system has only necessity, no ought — "should" is merely the phenomenal-layer description of necessary tendency. Analogous to "water should flow downward" — this is the manifestation of nature, not a normative command.

### 4.2 The Natural Gradient of Distinguishing-Intensity

From the angle of the act of distinguishing, different objects exhibit a natural gradient in the distinguishing-intensity that "I" can apply to them:

> Self (clearest) → those close in relation → those with shared ground → **powerful strangers who can significantly influence "my" capacity to form distinctions** → undistinguished strangers

The fundamental criterion for judging the gradient is "influence on 'my' total distinguishing-volume" — powerful strangers who can significantly influence "my" capacity to form distinctions (providers of education, information, opportunity) must be incorporated into the gradient consideration; although the distinguishing toward them is shallow, they influence the capacity for distinguishing through structural variables.

### 4.3 Gradient Ethics

The classical progressive structure "cultivate the self → order the family → govern the state → pacify all-under-heaven" conforms to the gradient derivation of the present theory.

**Core proposition**: Any utopian scheme that ignores the Distinction-Gradient and leaps directly to the "pacify all-under-heaven" level must necessarily violate human beings' actual personhood-capacity as distinguishers — the result is hypocritical power or failed utopia.

To cross the gradient is to violate the actual personhood-structure of the act of distinguishing.

### 4.4 The Placement of Good and Evil

The goodness of human nature is established by humans having the drive to distinguish. But where does "evil" come from?

Answer: evil is not "distinguishing itself", but rather **that which promotes the reduction of total distinguishing-volume** — monotonization, uniformization, suppression of difference, erasure of multiplicity — all tendencies that narrow the range of unfolding of the act of distinguishing are relative evil.

Evil is that which must exist but must be suppressed — because distinguishing is the Dao, any tendency that reduces total distinguishing-volume diverges from the direction of the Dao. The existence of evil itself does not deny that it is a node within the already-completed structure (under the complete perspective it is also part of the realization of the Dao), but as a personhood-responsibility of the distinguisher, the suppression of tendencies that reduce total distinguishing-volume is the distinguisher's internal personhood-responsibility.

Therefore, the goodness of human nature derives directly from the ontology of "distinguishing is the Dao": drives that share the source with distinguishing are good; tendencies opposed to distinguishing are evil.

**Good necessarily exceeds evil**: since the essential direction of the act of distinguishing is to exhaust all-that-can-be-distinguished (Thesis III), within the already-completed totality, nodes consistent with this direction (good = promoting the increase of total distinguishing-volume) must necessarily exceed in quantity the nodes opposed to this direction (evil = reducing total distinguishing-volume). This is not a normative command, but a structural fact — total distinguishing-volume must ultimately move toward exhaustion, so good must necessarily dominate. "My evil is also part of the well-distinguished" is true as a local proposition, but in the overall structure evil cannot become the dominant force.

### 4.5 The Personhood of National Law

National law's protection of undistinguished strangers is a **fallback mechanism**, not the ultimate goal — because when distinguishing-intensity is insufficient, fine judgment cannot be made, so a fallback is needed; but the real distinguishing within the gradient should be established first. The state is the necessary fallback when the capacity for distinguishing is insufficient; it is not itself the purpose.

The theory systematically presented in this paper occupies a metaphysical coordinate that had not previously been precisely named:

> **Action-ontology + already-completed-distinguishing determinism + Distinctionist Solipsism**

It is neither materialism nor idealism; it bears the force of determinism (all has been distinguished) while also preserving the real bearing of specific personhood.
