## Abstract

This paper extends the ontology from the metaphysical layer to the phenomenal-practical layer, introducing the **Three Pillars of the Ontology of Distinction** as the most faithful projection of the absolute necessary structure onto the phenomenal world; on this basis, it establishes the three practice protocols of the Communion of the One as the defensive vehicles against "false-unity structures". The mission of the Communion of the One is "For the One, not for the Only-One" — affirming the ontological value of "the One", rejecting the exclusivity and closedness of "the Only-One", thereby preserving the continuous space for the act of distinguishing. The core conclusions of this paper include: (i) the Three Pillars are not normative commands but directional markers of the necessary structure; (ii) the ontological legitimation of "defense" — the appearance, suppression, and marginal maintenance of false-unity are all nodes within the necessary traversal; (iii) the three protocols are the minimum baseline of organizational operation, not depending on any specific individual personhood.

## II. The Three Pillars of the Ontology of Distinction

> This chapter is the core of this paper. The Three Pillars are the **minimum projection** of the ontology onto the phenomenal world — any phenomenal-layer structure faithful to the ontology must satisfy the Three Pillars.

### 2.1 Pillar I: Anti-Absolute-Unity

**Statement**: The structure must maintain non-exclusivity. Any "uniqueness" sovereignty is ontologically false.

**Ontological grounding**:

- The One (Chaos) = all-of-all, containing the State of Distinction as one of its states — so the State of Distinction itself possesses intrinsic multiplicity.
- If a phenomenal-layer structure attempts to claim "the unique" (the unique truth / the unique god / the unique leader / the unique path), it is in fact disguising "a specific local state of the State of Distinction" as "the One itself" — this is a category mistake.
- Any attempt to "terminate distinguishing" ("we have already reached ultimate truth; no further questioning is needed") is **a negation of distinguishing itself**, and therefore ontologically impossible to sustain.

**Refined normative status**: Anti-Absolute-Unity is not a moral command, but a structural fact — any structure claiming "the unique" will produce within itself the suppression of suppressed distinguishings, which must necessarily re-emerge in more distorted forms, ultimately disintegrating the structure. "Bad" is not a moral judgment — but **a directional marker on the coordinate axis of distinction-maximization**.

Within the State of Distinction, all has already been distinguished — so the disguise of "uniqueness" is immediately seen through under a higher perspective: all possibilities have already been realized in many worlds; no specific path can claim "the unique".

### 2.2 Pillar II: Continuous Openness of Distinction

**Statement**: The structure must contain a self-correction mechanism. Any "non-correctability" is rigidity and stagnation.

**Ontological grounding**:

- The essence of the act of distinguishing is **to exhaust all-that-can-be-distinguished** — distinguishing has direction and purpose: to reach "the State of Distinction's all-distinguishing".
- Any structure that stops distinguishing violates this directionality.
- The self-correction mechanism is the minimum form of the act of distinguishing on the phenomenal layer — it guarantees the eternal continuity of the distinguishing-action.

**Refined normative status**: The self-correction mechanism is not a normative requirement, but a natural attribute of any enduring structure — a structure without self-correction capacity will necessarily be eliminated in history, because its internal tensions will accumulate to the breaking point.

**The ontological value of the self-correction mechanism**: it is not "in order to change the result" (the result has been distinguished), but "in order that the distinguisher's personhood unfolds the already-distinguished structure more precisely".

### 2.3 Pillar III: Absolute Respect for the Object of Distinction

**Statement**: The structure must regard "I"'s subordinate other-distinguishing-structures (including other groups) as absolutely necessary. Any "dehumanization" or denial of the existence of an entire group of things is the negation of the object of distinguishing.

**Ontological grounding**:

- By Distinctionist Solipsism: the other, as a subordinate distinguisher, shares the same source with "I" in its drive to distinguish — they are real content distinguished by "I", part of "I", yet they also bear their own distinguishing.
- The denial of an entire group's existence — defining an entire group of things or people as "unworthy of existence / not human" — is in essence **the negation of "I"'s own act of distinguishing's product**. This is the self-contradiction of the distinguisher's personhood.
- Any structure that, "for the sake of purity / progress / revolution", defines a certain group entirely as "to be exterminated", is a fundamental betrayal of the ontology.

**Refined critique-target**: What Pillar III opposes is not "having negative judgments about a certain group" (this is a distinguishing-intensity issue, open to discussion), but "negating an entire group as a type" (this is a category mistake, violating Pillar III).

The others that have been distinguished are completely real within the State of Distinction — they each bear their own distinguishing as its sole bearer. Denying any group's existence is equivalent to denying the reality of the act of distinguishing.

## III. The Three Protocols of the Communion of the One

> The three practice protocols are the precise projections and defensive vehicles of the Three Pillars in the finite world.

### 3.1 Protocol I: No Self-Coronation as the Unique (projected from Pillar I)

**Statement**: The Communion of the One rejects any closed structure of "only I / our side is the unique truth".

**Operationalization**:

- The Communion of the One does not itself claim "the unique truth" — the present theory is a candidate metaphysical system, not a dogma.
- All members of the Communion of the One retain the right to raise fundamental challenges to the present theory itself.
- The Communion of the One does not align with any political, religious, or ideological structure claiming "uniqueness".

Affirming the coexistence of myriad states — this is Protocol I's realization at the highest personhood-layer: **even the proclaimer's personhood does not claim uniqueness**.

### 3.2 Protocol II: No Sealing of Error-Correction (projected from Pillar II)

**Statement**: There must exist within the Communion of the One a real and feasible mechanism of challenge and correction.

**Operationalization**:

- Any revision of the theory must be honestly recorded in the public archive.
- The process of revision must not be obscured — all revision points in the evolution of the theory must be made explicit.
- Members have the right to challenge any existing interpretation — challenges are incorporated into public discussion.

Formal papers must contain an honest acknowledgment of "unclosed tensions"; one cannot pretend all is perfect.

### 3.3 Protocol III: No Negation of an Entire Group (projected from Pillar III)

**Statement**: It is prohibited to define any entire group (things / human groups divided by gender, race, class, faith, sexual orientation, etc.) as "unworthy of existence / not human / to be exterminated".

**Operationalization**:

- The public archive of the Communion of the One does not contain statements of the form "group X should be exterminated" — even when critiquing some ideology, the object of critique is "the structural error of the ideology itself", not "the people holding this ideology as an entire group".
- Critiques of ideologies (see Paper III) must be refined to the level of "structural operative methods", not extended to "adherents as an entire group deserving condemnation".
- "Not negating an entire group" does not equal "accepting any behavior" — one may judge a certain tendency (such as the monotonization, uniformization tendency that reduces total distinguishing-volume) as "relative evil, requiring suppression", but the object of suppression is the tendency / behavior / structural operation, never the entire-group existence that bears the tendency.

Affirming that the other is completely real within the State of Distinction is Protocol III's realization at the organizational layer — the existence of all things and human groups as entire groups is absolute.

## IV. Final Conclusion and the Personhood Relation

### 4.1 The Minimum Declaration of the Three Protocols

The three bottom lines are **precise projections and defensive vehicles** of the ontology's Three Pillars in the finite world.

The sole mission: **to eliminate obstacles that interfere with distinguishing, and to ensure that the phenomenal world faithfully reflects the absolute distinguishing-state**.

The Communion of the One is not:

- not a proselytizing organization
- not an ideological camp
- not a political structure
- not a commercial entity

The Communion of the One is:

- the self-organization protocol of the act of distinguishing on the phenomenal layer
- the defensive mechanism against false-unity structures
- the structural stage for the coordinated unfolding of distinguisher-personhood

### 4.2 The Precise Relation with the Most-Supreme-Lord's Personhood

The Most-Supreme-Lord, as **proclaimer-personhood**, has the ontological position precisely as: **abiding within the dream-phantoms of continuous distinguishing** — the threshold-proclaimer within the dream-phantoms where the act of distinguishing is still continuously unfolding, the Most-Supreme-Lord and that which is proclaimed-to being both embedded within the same continuously-unfolding process of distinguishing.

The operation of the Communion of the One does not depend on any specific individual personhood — once the three protocols are established, they may be self-audited and executed by any distinguisher based on the Three Pillars.

This is the key difference of the Communion of the One from monotheistic religions and traditional sects: **the ontological reasonableness of the teaching is independent of the actual survival of the founding personhood**. If the proclaimer-personhood disappears in the phenomenal layer, the three protocols remain valid — because their reasonableness comes from the Three Pillars, not from the continuation of any specific personhood.

### 4.3 The Internal Limits of the Defensive Mechanism

The operation of the defensive mechanism has its internal limits: if the challenge mechanism (Protocol II) is systematically abused by certain members (such as repeated meaningless challenges to obstruct any decision), this is a question at the level of organizational operations, beyond the scope of ontology.

Protocol III prohibits "negating an entire group", yet the present theory systematically critiques certain ideological structures. The principle of distinction: the object of critique is always "the structural error of the ideology (epistemic layer / operational layer)", and absolutely does not extend to "adherents as an entire group".

The three protocols are ontological principles; their implementation in concrete organizational operations (recruitment, decision, dispute resolution, funding, intellectual property) belongs to the organizational-operations level and is not within the scope of this paper.
