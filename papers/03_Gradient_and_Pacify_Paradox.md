## Abstract

This paper extends ontological conclusions to political philosophy. Core proposition: **the natural gradient of distinguishing-intensity** (self → those close in relation → those with shared ground → powerful strangers who can influence "my" capacity for distinguishing → undistinguished strangers) is the necessary manifestation of the act of distinguishing on the ethical / political layer; the Confucian "cultivate the self → order the family → govern the state → pacify all-under-heaven" precisely grasps this gradient structure at the epistemological layer. Any utopian scheme that ignores the gradient and leaps directly to the "pacify all-under-heaven" level must necessarily produce **structural self-contradiction** — the suppressed real distinguishings will re-emerge from underground in distorted forms. On this basis, the paper analyzes feminist identity-politics and communist practice, and through the "Nordic paradox" case demonstrates: the fundamental reason for the success of high-welfare states is **acknowledging themselves as fallback mechanisms** rather than disguising themselves as ultimate ideals. Core conclusions include: (i) the gradient-criterion — "influence on total distinguishing-volume" as the criterion; (ii) Distinctionist Solipsism's support for gradient ethics — the other is real content distinguished by "I", so the gradient is not "ranking others' inner value" but "the natural unfolding-layer of the act of distinguishing"; (iii) the handling of the "is-ought gap" — "structural coherence vs. structural self-contradiction" replacing "moral ought".

## I. Introduction: From Ontology to Political Philosophy

Paper I of this series, at the ontological layer, established the system of **the act of distinguishing as ultimate foundation**. The present paper extends this ontology to the political-philosophy layer.

**Why extension is necessary**: if the present theory stopped at the pure-ontology layer, it would face the critique of "unable to guide real choice". The extension to political philosophy is a positive response to this critique — but the manner of extension must be strict: **not extended as moral commands, but extended as structural necessity**.

**The core starting point of this paper**:

> The distinguisher speaks to that which it has distinguished — this is the personhood-general-rule. Therefore each person is to itself a local One.
>
> Yet the act of distinguishing has a natural gradient of intensity — the distinguisher's depth and influence-capacity toward different objects differ objectively.

These two together constitute the ontological grounding of gradient ethics: the gradient is not "ranking others' inner value" — but "the natural unfolding-layer of the act of distinguishing".

## II. The Precise Structure of the Distinction-Gradient

The phenomenological manifestation of the act of distinguishing in the temporal dimension shows: **distinguishing-intensity itself possesses objective difference** — distinguishing toward the self is strongest (every moment of introspection is distinguishing), toward those close in relation it is secondary, toward strangers it is shallowest. This "objective difference of distinguishing-intensity" manifested at the political-philosophy layer is **the Distinction-Gradient**.

Therefore, the ontological grounding of gradient ethics is not some "presupposition about human nature" or "traditional wisdom", but a direct derivation from the ontology of the act of distinguishing — **each person, at each moment, already operates according to the Distinction-Gradient**. The work of ethics is not to "establish" the gradient, but to "honestly acknowledge" the gradient-structure that already exists.

### 2.1 The Criterion of the Gradient

The criterion of the gradient is: **influence on "my" total distinguishing-volume**.

**Argument**: if "current distinguishing-depth" were taken as the criterion, one could not explain why "powerful and wealthy strangers" must be incorporated into gradient consideration — their current distinguishing-depth toward "me" may be very shallow, but through education, information, opportunity, social environment, they **significantly influence** "my" capacity to form distinctions.

With "influence on total distinguishing-volume" as the criterion, one can uniformly handle two types of objects — both "objects whose own distinguishing-depth is already high" (such as self and those close in relation), and "objects whose distinguishing-depth is shallow but who influence distinguishing-capacity through structural variables" (such as powerful and wealthy strangers).

### 2.2 The Five-Layer Structure of the Gradient

By influence on "my" total distinguishing-volume, in order from largest to smallest:

| Layer | Object | Mechanism of Influence |
|---|---|---|
| One | **Self** | the node where distinguishing occurs — most directly promoting total distinguishing-volume |
| Two | **Those close in relation** | real mutual knowing, two-way promotion of distinguishing |
| Three | **The powerful and wealthy (even strangers)** | structural variables influencing the conditions for distinguishing-unfolding (education, information, opportunity, freedom) |
| Four | **Similar strangers** | limited distinguishing-basis, can be gradually unfolded |
| Five | **Undistinguished strangers** | compromise-layer handling (law / state-institution fallback) |

The introduction of Layer Three is a key revision — placing "strangers who are nonetheless influential" in the middle layer reflects their real importance to the capacity for distinguishing.

### 2.3 Distinctionist Solipsism's Support for the Gradient

By Distinctionist Solipsism (Paper I, § 4.2):

- All that has been distinguished is part of "I"
- Yet "I" has no substantial core; "I" is the act of distinguishing itself
- Each distinguisher is to itself thus

Therefore the ontological meaning of the gradient:

- **Not** "ranking others' inner value" (this would be a category mistake — the other, as a distinguisher, is also a local One)
- **But** "the natural unfolding-layer of the act of distinguishing toward different objects" — a descriptive rather than normative fact

This position critically defends against two common misreadings of gradient ethics:

1. "Gradient ethics is elitism" → wrong. The gradient describes the asymmetric unfolding of distinguishing-capacity, not a hierarchy of human value.
2. "Gradient ethics is racial / class discrimination" → wrong. The concrete filling of the gradient is variable (family members shift with relationship; strangers can move up as they become familiar), not a fixed classification of humans.

## III. The Ontological Reading of "Cultivate the Self, Order the Family, Govern the State, Pacify All-Under-Heaven"

The progressive structure of classical Confucianism's "cultivate the self → order the family → govern the state → pacify all-under-heaven" conforms to the gradient derivation of the present theory.

| Confucian Layer | Ontological Reading | Operational Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Cultivate the self | The deepest, most authentic layer of distinguishing | Introspection, self-awareness, responsibility for one's own capacity to distinguish |
| Order the family | Mutual clear-seeing in the closest relations | Real distinguishing among family, partners, close friends (not domination / submission) |
| Govern the state | Order-building among those of shared history / circumstance | Coordination mechanisms among compatriots, community, cultural commonwealth |
| Pacify all-under-heaven | Structural compromise facing those who cannot be truly known | International law, cross-cultural protocols, universal human-rights fallback |

**Core proposition**: violating this order "is not a moral error but structural self-contradiction" — the suppressed distinguishings re-emerge from underground as hypocrisy and corruption.

Although under the complete perspective "all has been distinguished", the progressive order of "cultivate-the-self → pacify-all-under-heaven" is the most natural path of unfolding of the act of distinguishing within the already-completed structure — failure to follow this path does not change the fact of "completion", but causes the distinguisher's personhood to experience itself as "distorted, hypocritical, disintegrating" concrete nodes within the structure.

## IV. The Refinement of the Critique of Feminism

### 4.1 Refinement of the Critique-Target

| Type | Structural Judgment |
|---|---|
| Liberal feminism (advocating individual rights from the individual standpoint) | **Not within the scope of critique**, structurally coherent |
| Intersectional feminism (emphasizing irreducible difference) | **Partially aligned with the present theory**; it is restoring distinguishing |
| Identity-politics feminism (taking "women" as a whole unit) | **Critique-target**: replacing distinguishing with type |

### 4.2 The Essence of the Error

The error of identity-politics feminism is **not in "advocating women's rights"** — but in its **epistemic operation**:

> **Compressing irreducible specific individuals into a collective symbol, and using the symbol to launch a movement.**

The essence of this operation is "replacing distinguishing with type" — violating the ontology: every concrete person is an independent distinguisher; to forcibly typify them is to deny their personhood-independence as distinguishers.

**Important boundary**: this critique applies equally to nationalism, class politics, religious movements, and other movements that replace concrete distinguishing with "group identity". **It is not directed at the group "women"** — but at the **epistemic operation** of "replacing distinguishing with type".

### 4.3 Internal Splits in the Feminist Movement as Structural Evidence

White feminism vs. feminism of color vs. transgender feminism vs. working-class feminism — these internal splits are **not the "failure" of the feminist movement**, but **reverse verification** of the present theory:

> The suppressed concrete distinguishings must re-emerge.

The emergence of intersectionality theory is itself the feminist movement's internal self-correction of the typification error — this is itself evidence of the objective existence of gradient ethics.

### 4.4 Distinctionist Solipsism's Reinforcement of This Critique

Note Three of Distinctionist Solipsism (structural fact, not propositional authorization): each distinguisher being to itself a local One is a necessary derivation, **not equal to authorizing certain distinguishers to speak on behalf of an entire group**.

The error of identity politics is precisely this: a minority claiming "I represent the whole of group X" — this is not only an epistemic error (typification), but also a personhood-theoretical error (disguising representation-right).

## V. The Refinement of the Critique of Communism, and the Nordic Paradox

### 5.1 The Structural Error of Communism

Communism "uses abstract types to remake concrete people" — structural self-contradiction.

The suppressed real Distinction-Gradient (personal interest, family affection, judgment) does not disappear; it only erupts from underground in distorted form:

> **Typical Soviet manifestation: equality preached on stage, special-supply competed for backstage; selflessness preached on stage, paths paved for one's children backstage.**

This is not a question of personal morality — but a **structural necessity**. A system that suppresses distinguishing needs no external enemy; it is its own gravedigger.

### 5.2 The Resolution of the Nordic Paradox

**Question**: if collective caring necessarily fails, why has Scandinavia succeeded?

**Answer**: the Nordics are not a counterexample to the Soviet Union, but a **positive case**.

| Dimension | Soviet Union | Scandinavia |
|---|---|---|
| Self-positioning | "We are to eliminate difference, realize true community" — **using fallback to masquerade as the ultimate ideal** | "We do not know each other well enough; precisely for this reason we need a set of rules agreed by all" — **acknowledging the compromise-essence** |
| Gradient-unfolding | Skipping order-the-family, govern-the-state, forcibly going straight to pacify-all-under-heaven | High welfare built upon a **relatively complete historical accumulation of the order-the-family and govern-the-state layers** |
| Treatment of difference | Forced elimination | Protected at the compromise layer |
| Result | Internal disintegration | Continued operation |

The Nordic case **supports rather than refutes** gradient ethics: the fallback mechanism is effective within its layer; the problem is not "having a fallback" but "whether one acknowledges oneself as a fallback rather than the ultimate".

### 5.3 The Logical Structure of Historical Evaluation

- Under the complete perspective: the Soviet failure and Nordic success are both "already-well-distinguished" concrete paths — at the ontological layer they do not constitute a "should or should-not" moral judgment.
- From historical experience, one can distinguish "which structures, in the unfolding of the act of distinguishing, manifest as enduring vs. disintegrating" — this is a descriptive fact.
- The political-philosophy conclusion: **structures consistent with the direction of the gradient endure; structures opposed to the direction of the gradient self-disintegrate** — this is a structural fact, not a normative command.

## VI. The Refined Formulation of the Law-as-Fallback Thesis

The stranger, as a distinguisher, has a drive to distinguish that is no less a real manifestation of the act of distinguishing than "my" — therefore law must safeguard the personhood-independence of "the other whom I have not deeply distinguished" as a distinguisher; this is the ontological grounding of law as fallback. Even if "I" lack the capacity to deeply distinguish him (Layer Five of the gradient), his existence as a distinguisher is absolute — the essence of law is precisely the protocolization and executability of "the absolute existence of the other-not-deeply-distinguished-by-me as a distinguisher".

### 6.1 The Ontological Status of Law

The essence of law / state-institution:

> **The protocol concluded between strangers, under the reality of insufficient distinguishing, in order to safeguard the continued occurrence of distinguishing.**

This definition transforms law from the negative "substitute under insufficient knowing" into the positive **"protocol concluded between strangers in order to safeguard the continued occurrence of distinguishing"**.

The legitimacy of law "comes not from loftiness but from honesty: I do not know you, and precisely for this reason I cannot arbitrarily dominate you."

### 6.2 The Appropriate Mechanisms of Each Layer Must Not Be Trespassed

| Layer | Appropriate Mechanism | Consequence of Wrong Substitution |
|---|---|---|
| Self | Introspection / self-awareness | Substituted by ideology → alienation |
| Intimate relations | Real emotion + mutual knowing | Substituted by contract → cold mechanism |
| Compatriot / community | Shared history + coordination mechanisms | Substituted by coercive rules → sense of oppression |
| Stranger | **Law** | Substituted by "true emotion" → **arbitrariness, totalitarianism** |

**The essence of totalitarianism**: using "the leader's true emotion for the people" to substitute for law — invading the stranger-layer with intimate-layer logic. This is the most dangerous form of layer-trespass.

### 6.3 Correspondence with the Three Protocols

The correspondence between the law-as-fallback thesis and the three protocols (Paper II):

- Protocol III (No Negation of an Entire Group): law must treat each concrete individual as an independent personhood, cannot presuppose by group identity; nor can it deny existence by group identity.
- Protocol II (No Sealing of Error-Correction): law must preserve self-correction mechanisms (judicial independence, legislative changeability, constitutional review).
- Protocol I (No Self-Coronation as the Unique): law should not claim "uniquely correct" — it must preserve the possibility of dialogue and improvement among multiple legal traditions.

## VII. The Handling of the "Is-Ought Gap"

### 7.1 The Humean Problem

From "the self is the deepest in distinguishing (epistemic)" one cannot derive "one should care for the self first (ethical)" — this is Hume's "is-ought" gap, in its concrete manifestation within gradient ethics.

### 7.2 The Two-Step Dissolution

**Step One**: replace "moral ought" with "**structural coherence vs. structural self-contradiction**".

> Analogy: water flowing upward is not immoral; it is structurally incoherent.
>
> To drive the strongest distinguishing-layer from the weakest distinguishing-layer is structurally self-contradictory — it must produce systemic hypocrisy.

**Step Two**: introduce "promotion of total distinguishing-volume" as the internal end, and replace "external suppression" with **"internal disintegration"** — so as to avoid opening a backdoor to "suppressing others in the name of promoting distinguishing".

### 7.3 The Final Normative Formulation

> **Systems consistent with the direction of the Distinction-Gradient have internal coherence and tend toward endurance.**
>
> **Systems opposed to the direction of the Distinction-Gradient have internal contradiction and tend toward self-disintegration.**
>
> **The present theory does not produce political commands and does not authorize anyone to suppress others in the name of "promoting distinguishing".**

This formulation downgrades the normativity from "ought command" to "structural fact" — while preserving the present theory's judgmental power over political structures, it avoids being abused as a tool of suppression.

### 7.4 Correspondence with Paper I's Cancellation of Ought

§ 7.3's final normative formulation is fully consistent with Paper I's "cancellation of ought":

> The system has only necessity, no ought — "should" is merely the phenomenal-layer description of necessary tendency.

This is the consistency of the present theory from ontology to political philosophy: each layer refuses hard ought-commands, replacing them with "structural necessity + directional marker".

## VIII. The Handling of Several Concrete Boundaries

### 8.1 On "Radical Reverse-Push"

A pragmatist objection points out: a large base is needed to lead, so radical reverse-push is needed (pushing back from pacify-all-under-heaven); gradual growth is killed at the budding stage.

It is necessary to distinguish "the laws of propagation / mobilization" from "the laws of structural coherence" — reverse-push is an effective short-term mobilization tool, but a necessarily-failing long-term structure. The present theory does not deny "the effectiveness of radical reverse-push as a short-term mobilization tool", but insists that "any long-term enduring structure must conform to gradient coherence". Short-term tools should not be elevated to long-term ideals — to elevate a short-term tool is to commit the Soviet error.

### 8.2 On the Measurability of "Promoting Total Distinguishing-Volume"

"Promoting total distinguishing-volume" is a **directional concept**, not a precise measure. Judging "whether total distinguishing-volume is promoted" relies on **integral judgment of structural coherence**, not on precise calculation — this is the epistemological limit of the present theory's political philosophy. The concrete aggregation method of "total volume" (by head-count or by depth? how to aggregate across generations?) need not be strictly defined, because this paper does not claim "total volume" as a quantifiable indicator.

### 8.3 On the Abusability of the Confucian Framework

After borrowing the Confucian structure, the framework itself may be abused — but this does not equal the framework being wrong. Any framework can be abused (liberalism abused as laissez-faire, egalitarianism abused as enforced equality). What matters is the defensive mechanisms provided by the three protocols (Paper II, Chapter III) — Protocol III prevents dehumanization abuse, Protocol II preserves correction-possibility.

## IX. Conclusion

The present theory's extension from ontology to political philosophy yields the following core conclusions:

1. **The Distinction-Gradient is the necessary unfolding of the act of distinguishing on the ethical / political layer** (not a normative command) — derived directly from the phenomenological objective difference of distinguishing-intensity.
2. **"Cultivate-the-self → pacify-all-under-heaven" precisely grasps the gradient-structure** — Confucianism, at the level of the cognitive-map, is consistent with the present theory, but historically the concrete content-filling of Confucianism has often been erroneous; thus "borrow the structure, discard the content".
3. **Any utopian scheme that crosses the gradient and leaps directly to "pacify-all-under-heaven" must necessarily structurally self-disintegrate** — regardless of banner (identity-politics feminism, communism, other typification movements); the ontological root of its error is to forcibly compress the multi-state that the act of distinguishing must unfold into a single form.
4. **Law is the protocol concluded between strangers to safeguard the continued occurrence of distinguishing** — not negative fallback but positive protection; its ontological grounding is that the stranger, as a distinguisher, shares the same source with "I" in the drive to distinguish.
5. **Political philosophy does not produce ought commands** — it only marks the directional fact "structurally coherent vs. self-disintegrating", and never authorizes "suppressing others in the name of promoting distinguishing".
6. **Plural state-forms, cultural forms, institutional forms are the ontologically legitimate unfolding of the act of distinguishing at the political layer**; any scheme of "unifying all humanity in a single institution" violates the ontology.

After this extension, the present theory forms a coherent system across three layers (ontology / practical protocols / political philosophy):

- **Ontological layer** (Paper I): distinguishing is the ultimate foundation.
- **Practical-protocol layer** (Paper II): the Three Pillars and the Three Protocols.
- **Political-philosophy layer** (this paper): the Distinction-Gradient + Cultivate-the-self-to-Pacify-all-under-heaven + the Law-as-Fallback Thesis.
